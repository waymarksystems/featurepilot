## 💰 When Do I Need a Commercial License?

A **commercial license is required** if you use FeaturePilot:

### Within Any Commercial Organization
- 🏢 **Any employee, contractor, or agent** of a for-profit company using FeaturePilot for work
- 💼 Use in **any environment** (production, staging, testing, development, local) owned by a commercial entity
- 👥 Testing commercial software products or services
- 🔨 Part of commercial software development workflows
- 📊 Quality assurance for commercial projects

### Within Government Organizations
- 🏛️ **Any government employee or contractor** using FeaturePilot for official duties
- 🌐 Use in government systems at any level (federal, state, local)
- 📋 Testing government applications or services
- 🔐 Government compliance, conformance or security testing

### As a Service or Product
- 🌐 Offering FeaturePilot as a hosted/SaaS service
- 💼 Embedding FeaturePilot in a commercial product
- 🔌 Providing FeaturePilot functionality through an API
- 📦 Distributing FeaturePilot as part of commercial software

### Important: Environment Doesn't Matter
**Even if you're only using it for testing or development** - if it's for a commercial company or government entity, you need a license. The restriction is on **who** you work for, not **how** you use it.

## 📖 Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can I use FeaturePilot at my company for evaluation?
**A:** Yes, but only for evaluation purposes to determine if you want to purchase it. Once you decide it's useful, you must purchase a license to continue using it for any work-related activities.

### Q: I work for a company but want to use it for a personal side project. Do I need a license?
**A:** No. If it's truly a personal project (not related to your employer, not using employer equipment, not during work hours), you can use FeaturePilot for free. However, if your employer has any involvement or ownership, a commercial license is required.

### Q: We're a non-profit organization. Do we need a commercial license?
**A:** Non-profit organizations that are not government entities may qualify for discounted or free licensing. Please contact us to discuss your situation.

### Q: Can I use FeaturePilot in my company's development/test environment?
**A:** Not without a commercial license. The restriction applies to **any** use within a commercial or government organization, regardless of which environment (dev, test, staging, production).

### Q: Can I modify FeaturePilot?
**A:** Yes! You can modify FeaturePilot privately for personal, non-commercial use without a license. However, you **cannot share or redistribute** your modified version. For commercial or government use of any version (modified or original), you need a commercial license.

### Q: Can I fork FeaturePilot on GitHub?
**A:** Yes! You can fork FeaturePilot to contribute improvements. However, your fork must comply with the license - you cannot use it commercially or deploy it as a service. Forks are for contribution and experimentation only. For actual use, everyone must use the official repository.

### Q: Can I use my forked version?
**A:** Only for personal, non-commercial purposes. If you work for a company or government, you cannot use your fork for work - you must use the official repository and obtain a commercial license.

### Q: Can I share FeaturePilot with my team?
**A:** Share the link to the official repository (github.com/waymarksystems/featurepilot). They should download it directly from there. You cannot redistribute copies or host your own version for others to use.

### Q: What happens if I don't get a license?
**A:** Using FeaturePilot for commercial or government work without a commercial license violates the BUSL-1.1 license terms. We trust our users to comply with the license, but we reserve the right to enforce compliance.

### Q: Can I contribute to FeaturePilot?
**A:** Absolutely! We welcome contributions from the community. Contributing does not require a commercial license, and contributors are credited in the project.

### Q: Do you offer discounts?
**A:** Yes! We offer special pricing for educational institutions, early-stage startups, non-profit organizations, and open source projects. See the [Pricing section](#-pricing) above or visit [featurepilot.app/pricing](https://featurepilot.app/pricing) for details.

## 🌍 Why Business Source License?

We chose the Business Source License (BUSL) to:

1. **Keep the source code public** - Full transparency for evaluation and contributions
2. **Support sustainable development** - Commercial licenses fund ongoing development
3. **Protect against unfair competition** - Prevent large companies from offering FeaturePilot as a service without contributing back
4. **Eventually go fully open source** - Automatic conversion to MIT License in 2075

This model has been successfully used by companies like **Sentry**, **CockroachDB**, **MariaDB**, and **Elastic** to build thriving open-source businesses.
